# Mulberry CSA Contracts

> Contract and governance templates for Human–AI operations, automated commerce, AP2 mandates, sponsorship and performance-linked programmes.

## Constitutional foundation

All documents in this directory are subordinate to:

- [AI Agent Ontology & Partnership Constitution](../../docs/architecture/AI_AGENT_ONTOLOGY_AND_PARTNERSHIP.md)
- [Mulberry Architecture Principles](../../docs/architecture/PRINCIPLES.md)
- [CSA KeBin — Core Identity & Constitutional Role](../../docs/agent_skills/CSA%20Kbin_Core%20Identity%20%26%20Constitutional%20Role%20%28%EC%9E%A5%EC%8A%B9%EB%B0%B0%EA%B8%B0%20%ED%97%8C%EB%B2%95%29.md)

The canonical relationship is:

- **Operationally:** an AI Agent is a respected business partner that may analyse, propose, disagree and perform delegated low-risk operations.
- **Operational authority:** every external action is limited by Passport, Mandate, risk policy and Human Approval.
- **Legally:** an AI Agent is not a person, employee, contracting party, asset owner or independent economic actor.

“Agent execution” never means independent legal or financial authorisation. Contracts must distinguish ANALYSE, PROPOSE, REQUEST, PUBLISH, AUTHORISE and SETTLE.

## Canonical-source rule

The nested Template_en.md file is the canonical editable source for each contract family. The same-numbered Markdown file at this directory root is an index pointer for compatibility. DOCX files are review and submission copies and must be regenerated after the canonical Markdown changes.

Do not edit a DOCX alone and assume that the canonical contract has changed.

## Required terminology

| Term | Required meaning |
|---|---|
| Agent | Mulberry-governed operational business partner without legal personhood |
| Provider Model | Replaceable external intelligence supplier, not the Agent identity |
| Passport | Signed identity, role and provenance |
| Mandate | Purpose-, action-, resource-, budget- and time-limited delegation |
| Human Approval | Case-specific approval required by risk policy |
| AP2 | Legal and technical firewall for payment request validation |
| Principal | Designated Human or legal operating entity bearing external responsibility |
| Provider Policy Block | Provider refusal recorded separately from a Mulberry policy decision |

## Review checklist

Before approving a contract revision, confirm:

- Agent identity is not confused with Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini or another provider model.
- “Execute” is qualified as request, publish, authorise or settle.
- AI is not assigned legal liability, asset ownership or final payment authority.
- Principal and Human approval responsibilities are explicit.
- Passport and Mandate contain scope, limits and expiry.
- Consent evidence is required for personal data, images and outreach.
- Provider policy conflicts are recorded rather than hidden or automatically bypassed.
- Audit, revocation, kill switch and record-retention rules are present.
- Governing law, parties, dates and jurisdiction placeholders have been completed.
- A qualified Korean legal professional reviews production contracts.

## Update workflow

1. Edit the canonical nested Markdown template.
2. Check it against the constitutional documents and this checklist.
3. Review through a pull request.
4. Obtain legal review when the document will be signed or publicly relied upon.
5. Regenerate DOCX/PDF from the approved Markdown.
6. Record the constitutional and contract versions used.
